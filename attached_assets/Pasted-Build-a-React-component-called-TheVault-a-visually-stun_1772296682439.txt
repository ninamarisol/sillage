Build a React component called "TheVault" — a visually stunning, interactive glass perfume shelf 
display for a luxury fragrance app called Sillage.

VISUAL CONCEPT:
The shelf should look like a real, lit glass display cabinet — think high-end department store 
fragrance counter meets personal vanity. Dark, moody background (deep navy #1A1A2E or near-black). 
The shelves themselves are frosted glass with a subtle light source glowing from beneath each shelf 
edge, creating that lit-from-within luxury retail look.

SHELF STRUCTURE:
- 3 horizontal glass shelves stacked vertically, each holding up to 5 bottles
- Shelves have a glowing bottom edge (soft white or warm gold light using box-shadow/glow effects)
- Shelf surface has a glass/reflective quality — bottles should appear to have a faint reflection 
  beneath them on the shelf surface
- Between shelves, subtle depth shadow to create 3D layering

PERFUME BOTTLES:
Each bottle in the user's vault is rendered as a unique SVG or CSS-art bottle. Include at least 
5 distinct bottle silhouettes:
  - Tall rectangular (think YSL Libre)
  - Short round flacon (think Chanel No. 5)
  - Geometric angular (think Killian / Creed)
  - Rounded oval (think Lancôme La Vie Est Belle)
  - Wide square flat (think Tom Ford)

Each bottle should have:
  - A semi-transparent glass body (use rgba fills + slight gradient to mimic glass)
  - A colored liquid fill inside (color varies by fragrance family: warm amber for orientals, 
    pale gold for florals, clear/blue for aquatics, deep red for ouds)
  - A gold or silver cap
  - The fragrance name as a tiny elegant label on the bottle body (serif font, small)
  - A subtle reflection/shine streak across the bottle using a thin white diagonal gradient
  - Fill level visible — the liquid doesn't fill the full bottle, showing how full it is (75%, 50%, etc.)

INTERACTIVITY:
- Hover over a bottle: it lifts slightly (translateY -8px), a soft glow pulses around it, 
  and a floating card appears above showing: fragrance name, house, fill %, AI match score 
  (e.g. "84% match to your profile"), and wear count
- Click a bottle: opens a detail drawer from the bottom with full bottle info, wear log, 
  and option to "List on Exchange" or "Log a Wear"
- "Log a Wear" button triggers a mini spray particle animation — tiny mist particles burst 
  from the bottle top, then fade
- Drag bottles between shelf positions to reorder your collection (drag and drop)

AMBIENT DETAILS:
- Soft animated dust particles floating in the background (very subtle, slow-moving)
- A warm gradient light source appears to come from above, casting soft bottle shadows downward 
  onto the shelf
- Empty shelf slots show a faint dotted outline with a "+" icon to add a new bottle
- At the top of the component: user's name, their Scent Archetype (e.g. "The Velvet Dusk"), 
  and total bottle count ("14 bottles in your vault")

SAMPLE DATA:
Pre-populate with 8 sample bottles:
  1. YSL Libre EDP — 75% full — amber liquid — 84% match
  2. Lancôme La Vie Est Belle — 40% full — pale rose liquid — 91% match
  3. Maison Margiela Replica Jazz Club — 90% full — deep gold liquid — 78% match
  4. Chanel Chance Eau Tendre — 60% full — champagne liquid — 65% match
  5. Tom Ford Black Orchid — 30% full — deep burgundy liquid — 88% match
  6. Dior Sauvage EDP — 55% full — icy blue liquid — 72% match
  7. Creed Aventus — 85% full — amber liquid — 80% match
  8. Jo Malone Peony & Blush Suede — 20% full — blush pink liquid — 69% match

TECH REQUIREMENTS:
- React with hooks (useState, useEffect)
- All styling via inline styles or Tailwind (no external CSS files needed)
- SVG bottle art drawn in JSX — no image imports required
- Framer Motion for the hover lift, drawer slide, and spray particle animation
- No backend required — all data in a local useState array

The end result should feel like something you'd see in a luxury app prototype reel on Dribbble. 
Dark, glowing, editorial. Not a list. Not cards. A shelf you want to stare at.
